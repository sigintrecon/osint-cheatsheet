## 1. Purpose of This Repository

This repository is published exclusively for:

- **Educational purposes** — teaching cybersecurity, OSINT, and threat intelligence concepts to students and practitioners.

## 2. Applicable Law

### Pakistan — Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act (PECA), 2016

Under PECA 2016, several activities are explicitly criminalized, including but not limited to:

| Offense | Relevant Provision (Summary) |
|---|---|
| Unauthorized access to information systems or data | Section 3 |
| Unauthorized copying or transmission of data | Section 4 |
| Interference with information systems | Section 5 |
| Unauthorized access to critical infrastructure information systems | Section 6 |
| Cyberstalking (including surveillance, monitoring, or tracking a person without consent) | Section 24 |
| Spoofing and identity-related crimes | Section 16 / 26 |

Reconnaissance, tracking, profiling, or "investigating" any individual — public figure or private citizen — **without their explicit consent or without lawful authority** may constitute an offense under one or more of the above provisions, regardless of intent.

### International Considerations

Where an investigation, target, or dataset crosses jurisdictional boundaries, applicable law may also include (non-exhaustively):

- **Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)** — United States
- **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** — European Union, where personal data of EU residents is processed
- **Computer Misuse Act 1990** — United Kingdom

Practitioners are responsible for understanding and complying with the law of **every jurisdiction relevant to their activity**, not solely their own.

## 3. Ethical Standard

Beyond strict legality, this repository is maintained under a personal ethos of **"Work Ethically."** This means:

- Techniques are practiced in **isolated lab environments** (personal VMs, sanctioned CTF platforms, or authorized engagement scope) — never against live, non-consenting targets.
- Any vulnerability discovered outside an authorized engagement is handled through **responsible disclosure** to the affected organization, not exploited, publicized, or used for personal gain.
- Curiosity is not a justification. "I wanted to see if it would work" does not constitute authorization.
- Consent must be **explicit, informed, and current** — a prior relationship, employment, or public visibility of a target does not imply ongoing consent to be investigated.
